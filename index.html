The Language Technology and Society Research Group at the University of Groningen is dedicated to investigating the intersection of Artificial Intelligence and society, with an emphasis on natural language processing. Our research concentrates on developing computational methods for tasks related to argumentation, deliberation, and persuasion, with the goal of improving critical thinking and reducing bias in decision-making processes.

Our Courses

Language Technology Project

In this project-based course, students are going to work on a natural language processing challenge. A challenge is a practical task in NLP related to a particular research topic.
Project Text Analysis

This is an introductory course to processing large amounts of textual data. The basics of language processing will be introduced both from a theoretical and practical perspectives.
Introduction to Programming I

This course, together with the subsequent courses Introduction to Programming II and Advanced Programming, constitutes a basic course unit in the curriculum of Information Science, where the student learns to program.
